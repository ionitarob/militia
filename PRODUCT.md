# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sales team (Ventas) and admins at Ingram Micro Spain. They use this daily during working hours on macOS desktops and iOS devices, checking which public IT procurement contracts are open for bidding. The job: triage licitaciones quickly, decide which are worth pursuing, and track their status. Time-sensitive — they check before client calls or bid deadlines.

## Product Purpose

IMLiti aggregates and surfaces Spanish public-sector IT procurement contracts (licitaciones and adjudicaciones) sourced from official government portals. It replaces a tedious manual process of checking multiple sources. Success: a sales rep can open the app, scan what's new today, and identify relevant contracts in under two minutes.

## Brand Personality

Precise, trustworthy, fast.

The product operates in a domain that is inherently bureaucratic — public procurement, government portals, legal deadlines. IMLiti should feel like the intelligent antidote to that: clean, sharp, and reliable. It earns trust through clarity, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- Generic government/public sector software (dull, dense, dated)
- Dark-background fintech apps (not appropriate for a government-adjacent B2B tool used in bright office environments)
- Over-branded SaaS with gradients everywhere and hero metrics cards
- Consumer apps with playful rounded corners and bubbly UI

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity earns trust** — every screen should answer one question without noise. No decorative elements that don't carry information.
2. **Speed is a feature** — interactions should feel instant. Skeleton loading, not spinners. Press feedback at 80ms. Never make the user wait for animation.
3. **Authority without heaviness** — deep navy and white. The color palette should feel institutional and confident, not corporate-beige or startup-bright.
4. **iOS native, not iOS clone** — use CupertinoWidgets throughout, but push beyond the system defaults in typography weight and spacing to build a distinct identity.
5. **Gold as signal, not decoration** — the amber accent is used sparingly: one accent dot on the logomark, active states, critical highlights. It should read as precision, not warmth.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text). No color-only state communication. All interactive targets minimum 44×44pt.
